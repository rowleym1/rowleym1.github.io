The History of Malware | IBM

Site Author

IBM

Abstract

This website gives users a brief overview of malware’s benign beginnings up to critical modern times. It generally accomplishes this by going through the most significant worms, viruses, and ransomware in chronological order. The website’s timeline begins in 1966 when repeatable programs were simply that of a theory, but it then jumps to 1971 when the first computer worm was created. From there on, the website formats its timeline based on the most notable malware created and the chaos it caused, up to 2016. Post 2016, the website briefly emphasizes the extent of malware as a critical issue in modern times.

The History of Computer Viruses & Malware

Site Author

Zephin Livingston

Abstract

This website is similar to the previous one in the sense where it gives its users an overview on malware’s general history. However, unlike the previous website, it is not formatted like a timeline. Rather, it gives a more descriptive explanation of certain critical time periods; for instance: 1948-1971. This website does also touch upon critical malware released during this time period; however, it mentions several different critical malicious programs that the previous website failed to touch upon, such as the Code Red worm released in 2001. This website also goes more in depth on how malware advanced as both computers and–more specifically– the internet began to modernize.
